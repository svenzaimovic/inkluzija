The core quality of proposed strategies is that they introduce significant progress and improvements into the inclusive education system, with rational investments.

Inclusive education is not expensive
Although financing of inclusive practices is one of the leading issues for many governments, inclusive education does not cost more than the special preschool and education system. The average cost of schooling students with disabilities in specialised institutions is seven to ten times higher than attending classes in regular primary schools.

The strategies, on the one hand, structure existing good practices, but they also introduce innovative models of support through establishment of centres for development of inclusive practices, strong networking of all structures in the cantons working towards inclusion in preschool upbringing and education, and development of mobile expert team networks as one of the important functions of centres for development of inclusive practices.

Deinstitutionalisation
The strategies foresee state-of-the-art methods for supporting children and students with developmental difficulties, by transforming specialised institutions into centres for development of inclusive practices which will provide comprehensive support to preschool and education institutions, children with developmental disabilities and their families. Staff in the existing special institutions would be trained and reassigned to jobs in the new centres.

The strategies are based on deinstitutionalisation and, as such, explicitly promote the process of transformation of specialised institutions to act in support of an inclusive preschool and education system. Centres for development of inclusive practices should provide comprehensive support rather than replace the regular preschool and education system.
Model ljudskih prava
Assessment of children with disabilities is focused on identification of their potentials, needs and interests, thus dismissing the approach based on diagnosing their disabilities. Multidisciplinary mobile expert teams will be formed for assessment of potentials, needs and interests of the child, which represents one of the key changes in the approach to children based on human rights, thereby permanently replacing the ubiquitous medical model

Strategies are based on the social model and the human rights model, and focus on:

  • Potentials
  • Needs
  • Interests
  • Culture

Strategies equitably develop support to:

  • Children/students with disabilities,
  • Their parents,
  • Their family members,
  • Preschool and educational institutions,
  • Non-governmental organisations
  • All other stakeholders in the domain of inclusive preschool upbringing and education.

„Implementation of this education strategy will certainly organise and adjust education in Canton Sarajevo to meet the needs of children with developmental disabilities and therefore also European education standards in this, most sensitive segment.“ – Elvir Kazazović, minister of education, science and youth of Canton Sarajevo.

„Approximately 10% of BiH population has some form of disability in terms of their physical, sensory, developmental, mental or intellectual functioning. This justifies the need for adoption of this strategy and a systematic approach to development of inclusive practices to improve the system for early detection and support to children with developmental disabilities.” – Zlatan Muratović, minister of education, science, culture and sport of Tuzla Canton.

Every child has the right for education
Even now, 12 years after the start of systemic inclusive education in BiH, children with disabilities are still at a disadvantage in the BiH society. The right to regular education is guaranteed to every child by the International Convention on the Rights of the Child and the Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities as well as many legislative acts in BiH, however, the situation in practice is quite different. Implementation of inclusion is inadequate; regular education remains unadjusted to persons with disabilities and there is a lack of adequate support services for children with disabilities and their families.

Strategies and action plans
Strategies for inclusive education of children with developmental disabilities and the associated seven-year Action Plans are the most important documents to date, in the twelve years of implementation of inclusion in the Tuzla Canton and Canton Sarajevo. The strategies were presented in December 2017 in Tuzla and Sarajevo and were produced with support and in active cooperation between the Ministry of Education and Science of Tuzla Canton and the Ministry of Education, Science and Youth of Canton Sarajevo, in consultation with several hundred individuals involved in inclusive practices, organisations and institutions.

Sets of measures introduced by these Strategies

Development of a comprehensive support system for children/students with developmental disabilities in preschool upbringing and education. Find out more

1. SET OF MEASURE
  • Create standardised mechanisms for early detection of developmental needs and potential disabilities in children/students
  • Create mechanisms for early intervention, monitoring, counselling and other forms of support to children and parents in preschool facilities
  • Create mechanisms for identification, counselling and monitoring in primary and secondary schools
  • Create mechanisms to support learning in schools

Improving the quality of work of teachers and improving expert capacities in preschool and educational institutions. Find out more

2. SET OF MEASURE
  • Strengthen professional capacities in preschool and educational institutions
  • Develop a support network for inclusive education
  • Gradually transform specialised institutions to centres for development of inclusive practices

Development of support services for the families of children/students with developmental disabilities. Find out more

3. SET OF MEASURE
  • Educate, inform and strengthen the families of children/students with developmental disabilities
  • Sensitise the public about the importance of deinstitutionalisation and family life for children with developmental disabilities

Strengthening the role of non-governmental organisations in the inclusion development process. Find out more

4. SET OF MEASURE
  • Systematically raise public awareness of the importance of associations of persons with disabilities and associations working in favour of children with developmental disabilities
  • Organise and structure financial support to associations of persons with disabilities and associations working in favour of children with developmental disabilities

Reallocation of existing budgets and use of project financing to build and develop an inclusive education system for children/students with developmental disabilities. Find out more

5. SET OF MEASURE
  • Deliver training in developing project proposals for national, international, cantonal and EU funds
  • Actively use the capacities of the Fund for Professional Rehabilitation and Employment of Persons with Disabilities
