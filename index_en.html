The core quality of the proposed strategy is that it introduces significant progress and improvements into the inclusive education system, with rational investments.

Inclusive education is not expensive
Although financing of inclusive practices is one of the leading issues for many governments, inclusive education does not cost more than the special education system. The average cost of schooling students with disabilities in specialised institutions is seven to ten times higher than attending classes in regular primary schools.

The strategy, on the one hand, structures existing good practices, but it also introduces innovative models of support through establishment of centres for development of inclusive practices, strong networking of all structures in the cantons working towards inclusion in education, and development of mobile expert team networks as one of the important functions of centres for development of inclusive practices.

Deinstitutionalisation
The strategy foresees state-of-the-art methods for supporting children and students with disabilities, by transforming specialised institutions into centres for development of inclusive practices which will provide comprehensive support to education institutions, children with disabilities and their families.

The strategy is based on deinstitutionalisation and, as such, explicitly promotes the process of transformation of specialised institutions to act in support of an inclusive education system. Centres for development of inclusive practices should provide comprehensive support rather than replace the regular education system.
Human Rights' Model
Assessment of children with disabilities is focused on identification of their potentials, needs and interests, thus dismissing the approach based on diagnosing their disabilities. Multidisciplinary mobile expert teams will be formed for assessment of potentials, needs and interests of the child, which represents one of the key changes in the approach to children based on human rights, thereby permanently replacing the ubiquitous medical model

The Strategy is based on the social model and the human rights model, and focuses on:

  • Potentials
  • Needs
  • Interests
  • Culture

The Strategy equitably develops support to:

  • Children/students with disabilities,
  • Their parents,
  • Their family members,
  • Educational institutions,
  • Non-governmental organisations,
  • All other stakeholders in the domain of inclusive education.

„Approximately 10% of BiH population has some form of disability in terms of their physical, sensory, developmental, mental or intellectual functioning. Prejudice, stigma, lack of information and lack of availability prevent some parents from including their children in the education system. This justifies the need for adoption of this strategy and a systematic approach to development of inclusive practices to improve the system for early detection and support to children with l disabilities.” – Zlatan Muratović, minister of education and science of Tuzla Canton.

Every child has the right to education
Even now, 12 years after the start of systemic inclusive education in BiH, children with disabilities are still at a disadvantage in the BiH society. The right to regular education is guaranteed to every child by the Convention on the Rights of the Child and the Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities as well as many legislative acts in BiH, however, the situation in practice is quite different. Implementation of inclusion is inadequate; regular education remains unadjusted to persons with disabilities and there is a lack of adequate support services for children with disabilities and their families.

The Strategy and action plans
The Strategy for inclusive education of children with disabilities and the associated seven-year Action Plana the most important document to date, in the twelve years of implementation of inclusion in the Tuzla Canton. The strategy was presented in December 2017 in Tuzla and was produced with support and in active cooperation between the Ministry of Education and Science of Tuzla Canton, in consultation with several hundred individuals involved in inclusive practices, organisations and institutions.

Sets of measures introduced by this Strategy

Development of a comprehensive support system for children/students with disabilities in education. Find out more

1. SET OF MEASURES
  • Create standardised mechanisms for early detection of developmental needs and potential disabilities in children/students
  • Create mechanisms for early intervention, monitoring, counselling and other forms of support to children and parents in preschool facilities
  • Create mechanisms for identification, counselling and monitoring in primary and secondary schools
  • Create mechanisms to support learning in schools

Improving the quality of work of teachers and improving expert capacities in educational institutions. Find out more

2. SET OF MEASURES
  • Strengthen professional capacities in educational institutions
  • Develop a support network for inclusive education
  • Gradually transform specialised institutions into centres for development of inclusive practices

Development of support services for the families of children/students with disabilities. Find out more

3. SET OF MEASURES
  • Educate, inform and strengthen the families of children/students with disabilities
  • Sensitise the public about the importance of deinstitutionalisation and family life for children with disabilities

Strengthening the role of non-governmental organisations in the inclusion development process. Find out more

4. SET OF MEASURES
  • Systematically raise public awareness of the importance of associations of persons with disabilities and associations working in favour of children with disabilities
  • Organise and structure financial support to associations of persons with disabilities and associations working in favour of children with disabilities

Reallocation of existing budgets and use of project financing to build and develop an inclusive education system for children/students with disabilities. Find out more

5. SET OF MEASURES
  • Deliver training in developing project proposals for national, international, cantonal and EU funds
  • Actively use the capacities of the Fund for Professional Rehabilitation and Employment of Persons with Disabilities
